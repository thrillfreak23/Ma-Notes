Fear has turned into a numb but skinned feeling, as if fear were worn
out and mere vigilance had taken its place but a bare-reality barely
conscious sort of vigilance that goes down to the bone or rather my
core is on my surface and I am entirely visible and two-dimensional.
It feels too real, like reality as mystery, as something
not-understood, like the way you perceive with immediacy when you are
a child, without the surrounding chorus of one's own history, which
has been nulled and voided by pain and weariness and that
aforementioned fear which is now nothing more than a kind of undead
lichlike vigilance.

I wonder if this is what it feels like to be a more natural and normal
person, meaning one who is neither as autistic nor so very verbal as I
am.  A kind of purity of consciousness, one not overburdened with the
"chorus of thought".  ---Thought is not consciousness, not awareness.
And it seldom helps anything at all.  We are not our thoughts but we
are not our consciousness either, or perhaps it's better to say we
are not merely our consciousness. We are bundles of many properties
and parts. ---But is this what it feels like to be normal?  Immediacy
of emotion happens when one's chorus or history-consciousness is
dimmed to a point where it is too weak to mediate unto us what we
should feel about our feelings. "Don't be told what you want to want
and you don't be told what you want to need.  There's no future, no
future, no future for YOU."  To live without always second-guessing my
life, to think without second-guessing my thoughts.  The price of this
style of consciousness might be desperation.  ---Often I recall the
way life felt when I lived above the river, in the trailer. Not the
first time, in that first place, but later, in the second place and
time. Without hope, without recourse, I was paradoxically free and
easy. Somewhere deep inside, beneath all the pain and running
concurrently along with the pain, was this deep wonder at existence
itself, a deeper perception of reality and its aesthetic beauty.
Nothing more useless in everyway but for one way: the feeling of
aesthetic pleasure is enough to keep one willing to continue the
risible farce and harlequinade that is human life. ---What hogwash. I
don't know if any of that's true. But it might be.

Maybe the reason I often think back to two distinct times of my life
(childhood and Bryson City in my fifties) is this: they both
transpired, in part at least, within hearing of the Tuckaseegee River.
They both have that in common and I am often conscious of it.  I don't
know why.  The river's been a figure in my personal mythology for a
long time, and is obviously important to me.  I hark back to it like a
dying person harks back to their mother.  It was one of the first
things I clung to, for whatever reason: the sound of the river meant
everything was okay.

Is a person really a complete person, a total human being, if the
"worst" has not already happened to them?  Is the experience of being
destroyed the necessary condition of being an excellent human?  Isn't
this just some pop-culture, Game-of-Thrones type "rape is character
development" type bullshit?  God only fucks with a broken and contrite
heart.  And contrition etymologically is what happens when you
are thrown from your car and leave your face on the asphalt: it hurts
a lot, provided it doesn't just fucking kill you.  There is no God but
I know that that is the kind of God that would be worth fucking with.
Thus people too, since God is a person, right?  The people I want to
fuck with are the ones that understand what the "worst" is, how it
feels, how to act around it.  ---The "righteous" do not live in a
"world" where the "worst" is even real.  They are the boy in the glass
bubble, as if they were alllergic to reality.  Their aforementioned
"chorus of thought" is always there mediating reality to their
consciousnesses, and they fight to keep it always there insulating
them behind impermeable but ultimately illusory glass.  ---But to be
honest I don't know of any way in which I'm better that the
"righteous" except in this subtle aesthetic-perception kind of way.
Maybe that's enough, maybe that's all there is.
