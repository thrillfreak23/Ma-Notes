mem :: pronoun :: 1s I/me
memen :: 1p we/us

for _mem_ the combinative forms differ. 

_mem_ verbal subject ending:
-en :: 1s verbal ending

_memen_ verbal subject ending:
-emen :: 1p verbal ending


_mem_ nominal possessor endings:
-em :: 1s possessor, my
-emen :: 1s possessor (my) + -en plural

_memen_ nominal possessor endings:
-emen :: 1p possessor, our
-emenen :: 1p possessor plural, our (things)

Ambiguity or ambivalence here:
NOTA BENE: -emen 1s possessor + plural == "my things"
           and
           -emen 1p possessor == "our thing"
           ARE the same.

_kenetemen_ is thus ambiguous, it can mean our dog or my dogs
